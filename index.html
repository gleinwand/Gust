Privacy Policy for Gust

Last updated: June 7, 2026

Gust is a free browser extension that reloads your open browser tabs with a single click. This policy explains what information the extension does and does not handle. In short: Gust does not collect, transmit, or sell any of your data.

What Gust stores

Gust saves only your extension preferences, namely your chosen refresh mode (all tabs or targeted) and, if you use targeted mode, the list of domains or keywords you enter. This information is stored locally on your device using the browser's storage and is used solely to remember your settings between sessions. It is never transmitted to us or to any third party.

What Gust does not do

  • Gust does not collect, read, record, or transmit the contents of the web pages you visit.
  • Gust does not track your browsing history or build any profile of your activity.
  • Gust does not use analytics, advertising, or tracking services.
  • Gust does not collect any personal information.
  • Gust does not sell or share any data.

The extension reads tab URLs only at the moment you click the icon, and only to decide which tabs to reload (for example, to match the domains you specified in targeted mode). This happens locally on your device and is not stored or sent anywhere.

Permissions

Gust requests the following browser permissions, each used only for its stated function:

  • tabs - to see your open tabs and reload them when you click the icon.
  • storage - to save your refresh-mode preferences on your device.

Data retention and your choices

Your settings remain on your device until you change them or remove the extension. Uninstalling Gust removes the locally stored settings.

Children

Gust is not directed to children under 13 and does not knowingly collect personal information from them.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a revised "last updated" date.
